# Business Model Canvas
**Business:** Yeast Coast Culture
**Date:** April 2026

## Value Propositions
- **Dehydrated sourdough starter** as the hero SKU — easy revive, long shelf life, coast-rooted story
- Small-batch ferments and pickles with clear salt/brine specs retailers can trust
- Transparent batch notes and shelf-stable labeling — reduces buyer guesswork

## Customer Segments
- Specialty grocers and pantry shops (PA, NJ, NY) — primary
- Cafés, bakeries, and farm stands looking for retail jars — primary
- Regional specialty-food distributors — secondary

## Channels
- Founder-led wholesale outreach and tastings
- Distributor partnerships for broader grocery placement
- Farmers markets and maker fairs for awareness and direct feedback
- Social media (process-forward: starters, jars, ferments)

## Customer Relationships
- Hands-on education — revive instructions, brine FAQs
- Consistent fill weights and pH notes earn reorder trust
- Seasonal drops keep accounts merchandising fresh jars

## Revenue Streams
- Wholesale cases of dehydrated starter pouches (national footprint)
- Wholesale jars (kimchi, kraut, pickles) — regional cold-chain where needed
- Seasonal limited runs at slight premium
- Occasional direct-to-consumer bundles online (lower volume, higher margin)

## Key Resources
- Live starter culture library and dehydration SOPs (intellectual)
- Commercial kitchen / fermentation workspace (physical)
- Relationships with grocers, distributors, and market organizers (network)
- Brand voice rooted in the East Coast pantry (intangible)

## Key Activities
- Production scheduling across dehydration vs. jar SKUs
- Wholesale sales and account checks (pull dates, facing)
- R&D on seasonal ferments and pickle blends
- Compliance-friendly labeling and lot tracking

## Key Partners
- Atlantic Specialty Food Distributors (wholesale reach)
- Local farms for seasonal produce (ramps, apples, cabbage)
- Packaging vendors (pouches, jars, tamper labels)
- Shared kitchen / cold storage partners where needed

## Cost Structure
- Ingredients (produce, salt, spices) — variable
- Labor (production, packing, sales, admin) — fixed / semi-fixed
- Packaging and labeling — variable per SKU
- Freight and cold-chain segments — variable
- Broker or distributor fees — variable (% of wholesale)
