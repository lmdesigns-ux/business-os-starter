## Purpose

Help a cross-functional team name one messy workflow, map it as-is, and leave with shared language plus a short list of improvement priorities they can act on without a follow-on engagement.

This is the teaser offer tied to SKU **DG-WKS-HALF** — designed to demonstrate value quickly and open the door to a full service mapping engagement.

## Learning outcomes

By the end, participants will be able to:

- Agree on the boundaries of one workflow (start, end, who's involved)
- Produce a simple current-state map on paper or a digital board
- Identify 3–5 pain points and rank them by impact vs. effort
- Name one experiment or next step per top priority

## Agenda

| Time | Activity | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|

## Materials

- Sticky notes (3 colors: steps, pain points, ideas)
- Markers, painter's tape or digital board (FigJam/Miro)
- Printed workflow scoping worksheet (one page)
- Timer visible to room
- Optional: anonymized example map from a past engagement

## Facilitation notes

- **Group size:** 6–12 is ideal; split into two mapping groups if larger.
- **Scope creep:** If they want to map "everything," park other workflows on a parking lot sheet.
- **Power dynamics:** Have frontline staff place steps first; managers add after.
- **Debrief prompts:** "What surprised you?" "Where do handoffs break down?" "What would you try next week?"
- **Common pitfall:** Spending too long on tool/system names — redirect to what *happens*, not which software.
